Community & Resources

The Esperanto community spans the globe, offering many ways to connect, engage, and improve your skills. From local clubs to online groups, hereâ€™s how you can join and be part of this welcoming community.

Social Media Groups

Connect with Esperanto speakers around the world through these popular social media groups:

  • Facebook: Esperanto Group - A large group where members share tips, ask questions, and discuss Esperanto.
  • Reddit: r/Esperanto - A subreddit for sharing resources, asking questions, and discussing all things Esperanto.
  • Discord: Esperanto Server - A lively server for practicing, chatting, and joining voice discussions.

International Events and Conferences

Participate in Esperanto events around the world, where speakers gather to celebrate the language and connect:

  • World Esperanto Congress (Universala Kongreso): The largest annual event for Esperanto speakers, held in a different country each year.
  • International Youth Congress of Esperanto (IJK): A fun, youth-focused event with social and cultural activities.
  • Esperanto Days: Local events and workshops organized by Esperanto societies worldwide.

Online Forums

Join forums to discuss Esperanto, ask questions, and share experiences:

  • Lernu! Forum - A dedicated forum for learners and speakers of Esperanto on the Lernu platform.
  • Edukado.net Forum - Discussions for educators and advanced learners, with materials for teaching and practicing Esperanto.
  • Amikejo - A community platform where you can discuss topics and engage in language exchanges.

Language Exchange Platforms

Practice Esperanto with native speakers and learners worldwide through these language exchange apps:

  • Amikumu: Find Esperanto speakers near you or connect online to practice conversational skills.
  • Italki: Schedule one-on-one language exchange sessions or find an Esperanto tutor.
  • Speaky: An app that connects you with other language learners for conversation and practice.

Podcasts, YouTube Channels, and Media

Engage with Esperanto through media to boost your listening skills and cultural understanding:

  • Evildea (YouTube) - A popular channel featuring videos about Esperanto culture, grammar, and news.
  • Esperanto Variety Show (Podcast) - A podcast covering various topics for Esperanto learners and enthusiasts.
  • Esperanto TV - A collection of videos and programs in Esperanto.

Global Correspondence and Pen Pals

Looking for a pen pal? Write letters to Esperanto speakers from other countries for a cultural and language exchange experience:

  • Pasporta Servo: A global hospitality network that lets you stay with fellow Esperanto speakers during your travels.
  • Esperanto Penpals: Several online groups help match you with pen pals from different regions.
  • Language Exchange Groups: Find groups on Amikumu or Italki that focus on written correspondence in Esperanto.
